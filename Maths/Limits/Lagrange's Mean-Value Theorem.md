**Statement:** if a function $f(x)$ is
1. Continuous in the closed interval [a, b],
2. Differentiable in the open interval (a, b)

	then there exists at least one point $c \ \epsilon \ (a,b)$, such that $$\frac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a} = f'(c)$$
